French Novel Map
→

An assistant professor in the Department of French & Italian in UW- Madison requested an interactive map detailing spatial components of an experimental French novel, Thomas Clerc’s Paris, musée du XXIe siècle, le dixième arrondissement. The purpose of this map is to elucidate underlying patterns of the text that are not readily obvious when one reads the text in a linear fashion. Together with my partners, we created this interactive map showing the sequential path of all the subjective markers drawn from the novel’s beginning to its end. The subjective markers are the pairs of feeling or expressions and location that the author experienced when he walked every street, boulevard, square, and other public space, of the Tenth Arrondissement of Paris (where he lives) and later documented in his novel.

 

Tools: QGIS, HTML, CSS, Javascript, Mapbox, Open Street Map

Challenges: Georeferencing, Timeline Configuration
